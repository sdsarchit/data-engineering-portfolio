The Problem

Traditional Intrusion Detection Systems struggle to keep pace with rapidly evolving attack vectors and massive data volumes.

  • High False Positives: Legacy systems flag benign traffic, causing alert fatigue for security operations.
  • Slow Training Times: Traditional SVMs and deep learning models take too long to train on massive enterprise network logs.
  • Imbalanced Data: Standard algorithms fail to detect rare zero-day threats hidden among millions of benign packets.

Our Solution

A robust Ensemble Hard Voting mechanism combining three state-of-the-art tree-based classifiers for superior detection capabilities.

  • Ensemble Consensus: Combines predictions from XGBoost, Random Forest, and Extra Trees to eliminate single-model bias.
  • High Throughput: Tree-based models offer rapid inference times suitable for real-time packet inspection.
  • Accuracy & Reliability: Achieves 99.89% overall accuracy on the massive CICIDS2017 dataset with minimal false alarms.

Key Findings & Research Summary

Abstract & Dataset

The project utilized the CICIDS2017 dataset, containing over 2.8 million records and 78 features. After rigorous preprocessing, features were reduced to 47 critical network characteristics, enabling robust evaluation across diverse attack profiles including DDoS, Web Attacks, and Botnets.

Ensemble Paradigm

By leveraging a Hard Voting Ensemble, the architecture combines the distinct decision boundaries of XGBoost, Random Forest, and Extra Trees. This approach drastically minimizes the variance seen in single-model deployments, resulting in highly stable intrusion detection.

Performance Highlights

While XGBoost individually peaked at 99.91% accuracy, the Ensemble model delivered a superior balance with 99.89% accuracy and a flawless False Positive tradeoff. This consensus-based detection proved specifically resilient against obfuscated attack patterns.
