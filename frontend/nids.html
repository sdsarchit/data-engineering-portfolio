Research Context

Why Ensemble Learning?

Traditional single-model approaches have critical blind spots. Our ensemble strategy eliminates them through consensus-based decision making.

The Problem

Legacy IDS technologies are losing the war against modern, polymorphic threats.

  • Alert Fatigue — High false positive rates in signature-based systems overwhelm analysts, causing real threats to slip through.
  • Single-Model Fragility — A lone SVM or neural network develops blind spots, making consistent misclassifications on novel attack patterns.
  • Data Imbalance — With benign traffic outnumbering attacks 80:1, standard models are heavily biased toward the majority class.

Our Solution

Hard Voting Ensemble — three independent decision-makers, one unified verdict.

  • Consensus Detection — Majority voting across XGBoost, Random Forest, and Extra Trees eliminates individual model bias.
  • Real-Time Capable — Tree-based classifiers deliver sub-millisecond inference, enabling live packet analysis at enterprise scale.
  • 99.89% Accuracy — Validated on 2.8M records from CICIDS2017 with near-zero false alarm rate.

Research Summary

Key Findings

Core insights from the dissertation that validate the ensemble approach.

CICIDS2017 Dataset

Over 2.8 million labeled network flows across 78 features, reduced to 47 after feature engineering. Contains 7 distinct attack categories including DDoS, PortScan, DoS, Brute Force, Web Attacks, Botnets, and Infiltration.

Ensemble Paradigm

Hard Voting merges the distinct decision boundaries of three tree classifiers. This consensus approach drastically minimizes variance and eliminates single-model blind spots, producing stable, reliable detection at scale.

Performance Edge

While XGBoost peaked at 99.91% individually, the ensemble achieved 99.89% with superior stability and near-zero false positives — critical for production deployment where false alarms erode analyst trust.
